# Basic Examples

Examples that are simple to understand and suitable as starting points for new Mesa users.

---

### Color Patches
A cellular automaton model where agents adopt the most common opinion among their neighbors, producing emergent clusters of shared opinions across a spatial grid.

**Example:**  
[examples/color_patches](../examples/color_patches)

---

### Deffuant–Weisbuch
A bounded-confidence opinion dynamics model where agents interact only if their opinions are sufficiently similar, producing outcomes such as consensus, polarization, or fragmentation.

**Example:**  
[examples/deffuant_weisbuch](../examples/deffuant_weisbuch)

---

### Forest Fire
A cellular automaton model of forest dynamics in which trees ignite, burn, and spread fire through local interactions, illustrating emergent spatial fire patterns.

**Example:**  
[examples/forest_fire](../examples/forest_fire)

---

### Conway's Game of Life on a Hexagonal Grid
A hexagonal-grid variation of Conway's Game of Life demonstrating cellular automaton dynamics and pattern formation under alternative neighborhood rules.

**Example:**  
[examples/hex_snowflake](../examples/hex_snowflake)

---

### Rumor Mill Model
A simple model of rumor propagation where agents probabilistically transmit information to neighbors, demonstrating stochastic information diffusion.

**Example:**  
[examples/rumor_mill](../examples/rumor_mill)

---

### Shape Model – Basic Grid with Two Agents
A minimal visualization example demonstrating Mesa's web-based grid interface with static agents, custom shapes, and directional indicators.

**Example:**  
[examples/shape_example](../examples/shape_example)

---

### Termite Woodchip Behaviour
A decentralized coordination model where termite agents move randomly and interact with wood chips using simple pickup and drop rules, producing emergent pile formation.

**Example:**  
[examples/termites](../examples/termites)

---

### Population Model
A geospatial model where agents are initialized from raster population data and move across a geographic landscape while respecting environmental constraints.

**Example:**  
[gis/population](../gis/population)
